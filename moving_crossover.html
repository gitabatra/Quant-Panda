Moving Average Crossover

Moving averages are ideally one of the most popular indicators used by traders for technical analysis but there is a limitation that the moving average is a lagging indicator. This is the reason why traders do not completely rely on moving averages and generally wait for the crossovers which is the strongest possible signal they generate.

A crossover occurs when a short term moving average crosses either above a long term moving average which is known as bullish crossover or below which is considered as a bearish crossover.

  • A moving average crossover occurs when the two moving averages one with a shorter period and other with a longer period cross each other.
  • Moving average crossover identifies the trends but does not predict future direction.
  • This strategy is used to identify shifts in momentum and to determine entry/exit strategy.

Types of Moving Average Crossover

  1. Golden Cross
  2. Death Cross

Golden Cross

Golden cross is formed from a crossover which involves a stock's/security's medium term moving average(50 day ) breaking above its long term moving average. It indicates a BULL market and it reflects a major shift in market sentiments as bulls prevail over bears.
