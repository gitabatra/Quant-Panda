Moving Average Convergence Divergence(MACD)

Moving average convergence divergence indicator is a momentum oscillator developed by Gerald Appel in the late seventies. It is a trend following indicator that is based on the relationship between two moving averages of prices.
The MACD measures the convergence, or divergence, between short term moving average and long term moving average. Convergence occurs when two moving averages move towards each other, and Divergence occurs when the moving averages moves away from each other.


Interpretation

MACD is the difference between two exponential moving averages and both the EMA's are based on the closing prices. Exponential moving average is a type of moving average that places a greater weight and significance on the recent data points. It is calculated by subtracting the 26-day exponential moving average(EMA) from the 12-day EMA, to estimate the Convergence and Divergence(CD) value. A 9-day EMA of the MACD, called the signal line and is often plotted on top of the MACD, and it functions as a trigger for buy and sell signals.
